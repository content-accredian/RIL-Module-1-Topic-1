Lag Features in Production Forecasting

What are Lag Features?
Lag features use previous time step values as inputs to predict future values. In oil & gas operations, yesterday's production often influences today's output due to equipment constraints, pressure dynamics, and operational continuity.

Rolling Windows for Trend Analysis

What are Rolling Windows?
A rolling window calculates statistics (mean, standard deviation) over a sliding subset of data. In oil operations, a 7-day rolling average smooths out daily fluctuations to reveal true production trends and detect equipment degradation.

Normalization: Standardizing Data Range

What is Normalization?
Normalization rescales data to a standard range (typically 0-1). This is crucial when features have different units: production (barrels), temperature (°C), pressure (PSI), and water content (%) must be comparable for machine learning models.

Example: Well Performance Metrics Normalization

⚙️

Normalized Scale (0-1)
Normalized Value = (Value - Min) / (Max - Min)
Why Critical for Oil & Gas: Without normalization, production values (thousands) would dominate over pressure values (thousands) in distance-based algorithms. Normalization ensures all features contribute equally, improving prediction of equipment failures.

Scaling/Standardization: Zero Mean, Unit Variance

What is Standardization?
Standardization (Z-score normalization) transforms data to have mean=0 and standard deviation=1. Unlike normalization, this doesn't bound data to 0-1. It's preferred for algorithms like linear regression and neural networks used in oil production forecasting.

Example: Production Efficiency Standardization

Application in Well Management: When combining multiple efficiency metrics (thermal efficiency, mechanical efficiency, etc.), standardization ensures algorithms don't bias toward metrics with higher natural ranges. This improves anomaly detection for equipment failures.

Domain-Specific Features for Oil & Gas

What are Domain-Specific Features?
These are engineered features based on deep industry knowledge. Rather than generic features, they capture domain physics and business logic. For oil & gas, this includes production decline curves, water-oil ratios, GOR (Gas-Oil Ratio), and pressure differentials.

Example: Well Decline Analysis with Domain Features

🔍

Key Domain Features Created:
• Water-Oil Ratio (WOR) = Water / Oil - Indicates water cut increase (lower well productivity)
• Gas-Oil Ratio (GOR) = Gas / Oil - Shows solution gas depletion
• Total Liquid = Oil + Water - Combined production decline metric
Industry Insight: These ratios tell the story of well decline. Rising WOR means more water production (aging well). Declining GOR indicates loss of gas cap pressure. Experienced petroleum engineers recognize these patterns; encoding them as features helps ML models predict remaining productive life and optimize workover decisions.
